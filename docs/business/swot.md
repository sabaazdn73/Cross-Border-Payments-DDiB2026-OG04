# SWOT Analysis

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths**<br>Walletless for both sides, no crypto knowledge at all<br>Legal by construction: we hold nothing<br>Verifiable compliance trail nobody else offers<br>Real E2E demo on sandbox + testnet<br>No token: no MiCA/MiFID exposure | **Weaknesses**<br>We own no licence, no corridor, no liquidity<br>Entirely dependent on partners for the actual service<br>Thin margin on other people's margins<br>Team of one in practice<br>Our contribution is a layer, not a rail |
| **External** | **Opportunities**<br>Post-March-2026 dual licensing pushes firms toward agency models<br>Compliance-evidence is unaddressed across the whole table<br>Underserved corridors are real<br>Hedera Stablecoin Studio + full course technical support | **Threats**<br>Extremely crowded. Ripple, Stellar, Circle, Stripe/Bridge<br>Licensed CASPs (Due, BVNK) can add our layer trivially<br>Partner dependency: they can disintermediate us<br>Corridor access is licensing, which we cannot fix<br>USDT non-compliance narrows the stablecoin choice to Circle |

**PESTEL, briefly.** *Political:* de-risking has closed corridors; regulators want traceability. *Economic:* the buyer is fee-sensitive and the incumbents are large. *Social:* migrant and freelancer remittance is a durable, growing need. *Technological:* stablecoin rails are mature; the gap is evidentiary, not technical. *Environmental:* not material. *Legal:* [this section](../legal/regulatory-landscape.md) and [this section](../legal/why-this-is-legal.md), the decisive axis.
