# Potential Partners for Collaboration

A researched, named candidate list per region — not aspirational coverage claims. Verified as of mid-2026; this space moves monthly, re-check before relying on any specific detail.

## Africa

| Candidate | Coverage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Yellow Card** | 35+ countries, 50+ currencies | Visa/Mastercard institutional partnerships; May 2026 Mastercard deal extending into Eastern Europe, Middle East and Africa (initial focus: Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, UAE). Decade of licensed operations across Africa — a geographic compliance moat generalist platforms can't replicate quickly. |
| **Conduit** | 23 African countries, direct local rails | Backed by Circle, Dragonfly, Altos Ventures; $53M raised; $10B+ annualised volume. Widely cited as the deepest Africa-specific coverage in the category. |

## Latin America

| Candidate | Coverage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Bitso** | Consumer super-app, 9M users across the region | Exchange, stablecoin yield, cross-border payments; BVNK's own LatAm liquidity partner. |
| **Lemon Cash** (Argentina) / **Littio** (Colombia) | Regional neobanks | Serve the highest-inflation markets in the region with stablecoin yield products (7-9% and 5-8% APY respectively). |
| **dLocal Stablecoin** | Merchant-scale collection | Extends an existing payments network into stablecoin natively — relevant if collection volume, not just payout, matters later. |

## Asia-Pacific & South Asia

| Candidate | Coverage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tazapay** | 100+ countries, same-day payouts | Licensed: Singapore (MAS MPI), Canada (FINTRAC), Australia (AUSTRAC), United States. Pursuing further licenses across Asia, LATAM, Middle East and the Americas. |
| **TransFi** | 53 countries, deep Southeast Asia / South Asia | Dubai-based; $19.2M raised March 2026. **Explicitly serves Armenia** — converts stablecoins to AMD via regulated channels with local bank withdrawal, which is directly relevant to the Portugal-to-Armenia corridor this project uses as its working example. |
| **Due** | APAC, Africa, LATAM from one integration | Widest local-rail access among independent providers per comparative industry reviews; Americas (7 countries incl. US, Brazil, Mexico), APAC (6 countries incl. India, Philippines, Singapore). |

## Middle East

| Candidate | Coverage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **TransFi** | Dubai HQ | Same provider as above — MENA is its home region. |
| **Plasma One** | Middle East rollout (launched June 2026) | British stablecoin neobank on a purpose-built Layer 1, backed by Bitfinex and Peter Thiel. Early-stage; worth monitoring, not yet a proven integration partner. |
| **Yellow Card** | UAE (via the Mastercard partnership above) | Entry point into the region rather than deep native coverage yet. |

## Institutional / infrastructure layer (cross-region)

| Candidate | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **Circle / Circle Payments Network (CPN)** | Issues USDC directly; deepest liquidity and regulatory standing in the US and EU. Explicitly thinner in Africa, LatAm, and Southeast Asia by Circle's own positioning — a reason to pair with a regional specialist rather than rely on Circle alone outside its core markets. |
| **Rhino.fi** | Infrastructure layer — Smart Deposit Addresses, deterministic multi-chain settlement. Powers Blockradar's wallet APIs for fintechs across Africa and LatAm, an indirect route into those regions. |
| **Ripple Payments** | 75+ money-transmitter-equivalent licences globally; pursuing e-money licences in Europe and the UK. Relevant if a bank-grade, heavily-licensed single counterparty is preferred over a multi-regional patchwork. |

## The honest summary

No single provider above covers every corridor this platform lists. That is the expected state of the market, not a gap unique to this project — even the largest named players (BVNK, Due, Tazapay) explicitly pair with regional specialists outside their home markets. A real partnership strategy would combine 2-3 of the above rather than searching for one provider that does everything.
